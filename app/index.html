He Said/She Said is a participatory, transmedia project about modern dating. Season One chronicles the lives of three New York couples through the digital breadcrumbs they leave behind. Public interviews and private messages reveal intimate details surrounding their patterns of courtship, day-to-day, and breakup travails.

What happens next you must be wondering...

In early 2015, I set out to document how couples conduct their online and offline relationships. I found three New York couples whose honesty and transparency made possible the content of this immersive storytelling project.

As a filmmaker, I’m interested in the degrees to which representations of identities are performed and enhanced through the stories we tell ourselves and each other. In this age of ubiquitous self-documentation, I suspect there are moments when we come to doubt the things we say or don’t say. That last posting you wrote - was it real? Or did it feel right in the moment?

I wonder in those moments: Are we cutting corners by forcing our experience to fit our language - language fitting enough to draw an invisible sea of nods and approval?

While the methodical emphasis in this project is on what we say, the true intention lies in turning the familiar tools we use into means of inquiry to deepen our understanding of the what is meant.
